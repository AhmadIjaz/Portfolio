MCP + RAG agent system for the dev loop

n8n · mcp · rag · pinecone

Deployment pipeline at scale

Shipping Glue to 500+ clients across multiple scaled servers was slow and fragile. I designed its deployment pipeline on BitBucket Pipelines with each step isolated in its own Docker container and the build defined in YAML; native iOS/Android builds ship through Ionic AppFlow. Google Food Ordering — crisis recovery

resilience · product strategy

DeliverLogic ran a direct Google Food Ordering (GFO) integration that served restaurants natively on Google's ordering page — peaking at 100K–110K orders/month. In July 2024 Google ended native ordering, switching to a redirect-only model where restaurants set a "Preferred by Business" destination; that native channel dropped to zero overnight. I designed the recovery to fit the new model: a redirect "glue" mechanism, branded through ActiveMenus, that auto-detects takeout vs. delivery intent and passes context to the client's site via query params, minimizing clicks. Combined with lower rates and fewer steps than competitors' redirect links, it recovered the channel to ~10K orders/month.

When Google ended native Food Ordering in July 2024 — shifting to a redirect-only model — a channel doing 100K+ orders/month collapsed to zero overnight. As lead developer and project manager on the response, I scoped and delivered a redirect-based recovery — an ActiveMenus-branded glue mechanism that detects order intent and routes customers to the client's site in as few clicks as possible. Positioned on lower rates and a smoother flow than competitors, it recovered the channel to ~10K orders/month. A resilience-and-product-thinking story as much as a technical one.
